Endnotes (Note 138)

Commentary

Summary

Endnote 138: Soviet control of information through photocopiers. Michael Parks, 'Soviets Free the Dreaded Photocopier' (Los Angeles Times, October 5, 1989).

Key Patterns

The Soviet Union's control of photocopiers symbolizes how authoritarian organizations hoard information. The authors use this as a cautionary tale against corporate information hoarding.

Practical Applications

Information hoarding is a form of organizational authoritarianism. Default to sharing information widely and freely. The benefits of transparency almost always outweigh the risks of leaks.
