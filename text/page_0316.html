Endnotes (Note 154)

Commentary

Summary

Endnote 154 explains the Apache license, written by the Apache Software Foundation. The license allows users to use, distribute, or modify the software under its terms without paying royalties, making it one of the most permissive open-source licenses available.

Key Patterns

The reference to open-source licensing connects to the book's broader theme of how openness drives innovation. Google's embrace of open-source software -- both using and contributing to it -- is a core part of their technology strategy. Open platforms attract more developers, generate more innovation, and ultimately create more value than closed alternatives.

Practical Applications

Understand the strategic implications of open-source licensing for your technology decisions. Open-source can accelerate development, reduce costs, and attract talent. Consider how contributing to open-source projects can benefit your organization by building community goodwill, attracting smart creatives, and driving adoption of your technology ecosystem.
