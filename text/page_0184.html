Commentary

Summary

Jonathan Rosenberg first met Larry Page and Sergey Brin in 2000 and joined as senior vice president running the Google product team until April 2011. He oversaw design, development, and evolution of Google's products for consumers, advertisers, and partners. Today he is an advisor to Google CEO Larry Page. Alan Eagle has been a director of executive communications at Google since 2007, leading speechwriting for Eric and Jonathan. He holds a computer science degree from Dartmouth and an MBA from Wharton.

Key Patterns

Jonathan's path from Excite@Home and Apple to Google demonstrates how smart creatives move between companies to find the right environment. Alan Eagle's communications role highlights the importance of storytelling and narrative in technology companies.

Practical Applications

Build a career that combines diverse industry experiences. Value the role of communications in translating technical excellence into organizational direction. Be willing to take risks on unproven companies if the founders and vision are compelling.
