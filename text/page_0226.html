Endnotes: Multisided Markets

Commentary

Summary

Endnote 66 defines multisided markets as places "where two or more distinct user groups can connect and provide each other with beneficial services." Examples include newspapers (connecting readers and advertisers) and credit cards (connecting consumers and merchants). The source is Eisenmann, Parker, and Van Alstyne's "Strategies for Two-Sided Markets" (Harvard Business Review, October 2006).

Key Patterns

Understanding multisided markets is essential to understanding Google's business model -- Search connects users with information and advertisers with audiences simultaneously. Multisided markets create powerful network effects where each additional participant on one side increases value for the other. The authors ground their practical insights in established economic theory.

Practical Applications

Evaluate whether your business could benefit from a multisided market model -- connecting distinct user groups can create powerful network effects and defensible competitive positions. When building platforms, focus on creating value for all sides of the market, not just the paying customer.
