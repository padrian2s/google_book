Commentary

Summary

Endnote 88: Jonathan's courtship with roses and puzzle. He sent her roses and a puzzle -- the roses to pique her curiosity, and the puzzle to determine if she was smart. She was, but went out with Jonathan anyway.

Key Patterns

This personal anecdote reveals the authors' personalities and their genuine belief that intellectual curiosity and problem-solving ability are attractive qualities in every context, not just business.

Practical Applications

Look for intellectual curiosity and problem-solving instincts in people, whether hiring or building relationships. These traits are more predictive of long-term success than credentials alone.
