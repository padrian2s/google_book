Endnotes: Achieving Failure

Commentary

Summary

Endnote 55 introduces Eric Ries's concept of "achieving failure" from The Lean Startup (Crown Business/Random House, 2011), pages 22 and 38. Ries defines this as "successfully executing a plan that leads nowhere because the plan was utterly flawed." This paradox -- succeeding at executing a doomed strategy -- connects to the book's emphasis on adaptability over rigid planning.

Key Patterns

"Achieving failure" warns that flawless execution of a bad plan is worse than imperfect execution of a good one -- strategy must precede operational excellence. Ries's lean startup methodology, with its emphasis on rapid iteration and validated learning, aligns with Google's approach to product development.

Practical Applications

Regularly question whether you are executing the right plan, not just executing well -- operational excellence without strategic validity is "achieving failure." Build validation checkpoints into your planning process to ensure you are not efficiently marching toward the wrong destination.
