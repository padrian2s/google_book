Endnotes (Note 176)

Commentary

Summary

Endnote 176 references Henry Ford's autobiography My Life and Work (1922), specifically page 147. Ford, the pioneer of mass production and the assembly line, is cited as an example of transformative industrial innovation that changed how entire industries operate.

Key Patterns

The reference to Henry Ford connects the book's modern tech-industry advice to a longer tradition of industrial innovation. Ford's approach -- making products accessible to the masses through efficiency and scale -- parallels Google's strategy of providing services freely to billions of users. Both models prioritize scale and accessibility as drivers of value creation.

Practical Applications

Look for opportunities to democratize access to products and services through technology and scale. Ford made automobiles accessible to the average worker; Google made information accessible to anyone with an Internet connection. Consider how your innovations can reach the broadest possible audience, as scale often unlocks new business models and competitive advantages.
