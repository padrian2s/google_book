A Note About the Authors

Commentary

Summary

Eric Schmidt joined Google in 2001 and helped grow it from a Silicon Valley startup to a global technology leader. As executive chairman, he is responsible for building partnerships, government outreach, and technology thought leadership. He served as CEO from 2001 to 2011 and previously held leadership roles at Novell and Sun Microsystems. He holds degrees from Princeton and UC Berkeley. Jonathan Rosenberg first met Larry Page and Sergey Brin in 2000, finally accepting a job in 2002. He served as SVP running the product team until April 2011. Alan Eagle has been a director of executive communications at Google since 2007.

Key Patterns

The author bios reinforce the book's credibility by establishing the authors' deep experience both inside and outside Google. Eric's background at Sun and Novell provides perspective on what traditional tech companies do wrong. Jonathan's experience at Excite@Home and Apple provides counterpoints to Google's approach. Alan Eagle's role in executive communications explains the book's emphasis on clear, authentic communication as a leadership tool.

Practical Applications

Draw on diverse career experiences to bring perspective to your current role. The authors' effectiveness at Google was enhanced by having seen what didn't work at other companies. Seek leadership roles at multiple organizations to build a broader perspective on what makes great companies.
