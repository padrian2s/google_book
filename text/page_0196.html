Endnotes: Google's Ads Network

Commentary

Summary

Endnote 36 defines Google's ads network product -- the system where Google places advertisements on a large network of publisher sites (known as AdSense/Google Display Network). This brief clarification helps readers understand the revenue model discussed in the main text.

Key Patterns

The authors continue to demystify Google's revenue mechanics, ensuring readers understand how the company generates income beyond search ads. The publisher network model illustrates platform thinking -- creating value by connecting advertisers with publishers at scale.

Practical Applications

Understand all revenue streams in your business, not just the most visible ones -- secondary channels can be significant. Consider platform models that create value by connecting multiple parties rather than serving just one customer type.
