Endnotes: HP's Little Basin Campsite

Commentary

Summary

Endnote 29 provides the history of HP's Little Basin campsite. In 2007, HP sold the site to two nonprofits -- Sempervirens Fund and Peninsula Open Space Trust -- who then sold it to the California state parks department. Today it is part of Big Basin Redwoods State Park and open for public use, as reported by Paul Rogers in the San Jose Mercury News (January 14, 2011).

Key Patterns

HP's campsite retreat exemplifies how companies can build culture through shared experiences outside the office. The campsite's transition from corporate retreat to public park illustrates how company traditions can have lasting community impact.

Practical Applications

Create shared experiences outside the office to strengthen team bonds and reinforce culture -- retreats, offsites, and informal gatherings build relationships that improve collaboration. Consider how your organization's physical spaces and traditions contribute to culture-building.
