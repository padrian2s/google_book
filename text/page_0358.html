Endnotes -- DARPA's Special Forces Innovation Model

Commentary

Summary

This endnote (196) references Regina E. Dugan and Kaigham J. Gabriel's Harvard Business Review article "'Special Forces' Innovation: How DARPA Attacks Problems" (October 2013). The article examines DARPA's distinctive approach to tackling ambitious, high-risk research challenges through small, elite teams with clear missions and tight timelines.

Key Patterns

DARPA's model of innovation -- small teams, big ambitions, tight deadlines, and tolerance for failure -- mirrors many of the principles the book advocates for Google and other technology companies. The "special forces" metaphor emphasizes quality over quantity in team composition, a theme that runs throughout the Talent chapter of the book.

Practical Applications

For breakthrough innovation, consider assembling small, dedicated teams with clear mandates and aggressive timelines. Remove bureaucratic barriers and give these teams the autonomy to operate like special forces -- fast, focused, and empowered to make decisions without excessive oversight.
