Endnotes: True Innovation

Commentary

Summary

Endnote 35 cites Jon Gertner's "True Innovation" (New York Times, February 25, 2012), an article examining what constitutes genuine innovation versus incremental improvement. This reference supports the book's distinction between transformative innovation and mere iteration.

Key Patterns

The authors draw on external commentary to sharpen the distinction between true innovation (creating something fundamentally new) and incremental improvement. Citing respected journalism sources like the New York Times adds public credibility to the book's arguments about innovation.

Practical Applications

Regularly assess whether your organization is pursuing true innovation or merely incremental improvements -- both have value, but confusing them can lead to strategic mistakes. Define what "innovation" means in your context so teams have a shared understanding of ambition level.
