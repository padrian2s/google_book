Commentary

Summary

Endnote 80: Henry Chesbrough on open innovation. Henry Chesbrough coined the concept of 'open innovation,' arguing companies should use external as well as internal ideas. His books Open Innovation (2003) and Open Business Models (2006) are foundational texts.

Key Patterns

The open innovation paradigm shifts the locus of innovation from purely internal R&D to a permeable boundary where ideas flow in and out. This aligns with Google's philosophy of openness.

Practical Applications

Evaluate whether your organization's innovation model is too insular. Consider structured ways to incorporate external ideas, partnerships, and even competitor insights into your innovation pipeline.
