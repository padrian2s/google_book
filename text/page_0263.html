Endnotes (Note 103)

Commentary

Summary

Endnote 103: Google's unconscious bias training. Google invested in training employees to spot their own unconscious biases. See Greenwald and Banaji's foundational work on implicit social cognition (Psychological Review, 1995).

Key Patterns

Addressing unconscious bias requires both awareness and structural solutions. Google's investment in training acknowledges that even well-intentioned people carry biases that affect decisions.

Practical Applications

Implement structured processes for hiring and evaluation that minimize the impact of unconscious bias. Training alone is insufficient without systemic changes to decision-making processes.
