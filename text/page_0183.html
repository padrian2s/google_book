A Note About the Authors

Commentary

Summary

Eric Schmidt joined Google in 2001 and helped grow it from a startup to a global leader in technology. As executive chairman, he is responsible for Google's external matters including partnerships, government outreach, and technology thought leadership. He served as CEO from 2001 to 2011. He holds degrees from Princeton and UC Berkeley and serves on several advisory boards including the President's Council of Advisors on Science and Technology.

Key Patterns

Eric's background combines deep technical expertise (Unix, Java) with business leadership (Novell CEO, Sun executive), embodying the "smart creative" archetype the book describes.

Practical Applications

Combine technical depth with business acumen for maximum leadership effectiveness. Transition between roles to build diverse perspective and skills.
