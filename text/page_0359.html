Endnotes -- Curiosity and the Toddler Mindset

Commentary

Summary

This brief, humorous endnote (197) observes that Dan (likely referring to a colleague mentioned in the main text) shares a lot in common with toddlers -- presumably in his relentless curiosity, persistence, and willingness to ask "why" repeatedly.

Key Patterns

The humor in comparing an adult professional to a toddler highlights a serious point: the qualities that make toddlers exhausting -- endless curiosity, refusal to accept superficial answers, willingness to try things even when they might fail -- are exactly the qualities that drive innovation. The best smart creatives retain this childlike drive to understand and experiment.

Practical Applications

Encourage a culture of relentless questioning. When someone asks "why?" five times in a row, they are not being difficult -- they are getting to root causes. Foster the toddler-like qualities of curiosity and persistence in your teams, even when it feels uncomfortable.
