Endnotes (Note 156)

Commentary

Summary

Endnote 156 explains the distinction between client-based and browser-based applications. Client-based applications use software programs residing on the computer or device, while browser-based applications reside entirely on the Internet and are accessed through browsers like Internet Explorer, Firefox, Safari, or Chrome.

Key Patterns

This technical explanation connects to one of the book's fundamental strategic insights: the shift from client-based to browser-based (cloud) computing represents a platform transition that reshapes entire industries. Google's bet on the browser as the primary computing platform (evidenced by Chrome and Chrome OS) was a strategic manifestation of this understanding.

Practical Applications

Understand how platform shifts create both threats and opportunities for your business. The ongoing migration to cloud-based and browser-based computing continues to reshape software development, distribution, and business models. Build strategies that leverage these platform shifts rather than defending against them.
