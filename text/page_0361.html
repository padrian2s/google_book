Endnotes -- The Bobby Ewing Problem in Business

Commentary

Summary

This endnote (199) references the infamous plot twist from the 1980s TV show "Dallas," where character Bobby Ewing died tragically when his sister-in-law ran him down with a car, only to miraculously return to life the following season -- his death turned out to be "just a dream." The authors wryly note: "We should all be so lucky."

Key Patterns

This pop culture reference likely illustrates the impossibility of undoing bad decisions in the real world. Unlike a TV show, business mistakes cannot simply be retconned away. The humorous tone is characteristic of the book's approach to making serious points through entertaining cultural references, keeping the material accessible and engaging.

Practical Applications

Accept that business decisions have real, lasting consequences that cannot be undone with a narrative trick. This underscores the importance of thoughtful decision-making processes, even in fast-moving environments. When mistakes happen, deal with them honestly rather than wishing them away.
