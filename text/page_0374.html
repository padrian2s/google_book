Endnotes -- Human-Computer Chess Collaboration

Commentary

Summary

This endnote (212) references Christopher Chabris and David Goodman's Wall Street Journal article "Chess-Championship Results Show Powerful Role of Computers" (November 22, 2013). The article discusses how computer-assisted chess (centaur chess) demonstrates that the combination of human judgment and computer calculation produces results superior to either alone.

Key Patterns

The chess collaboration example illustrates a key theme in the book's conclusion: the future belongs not to humans or computers alone, but to effective human-computer partnerships. This "centaur" model -- where human creativity and intuition complement machine processing power -- represents the authors' vision for how smart creatives will work in the future.

Practical Applications

Design workflows that leverage the unique strengths of both humans and computers. Humans excel at creativity, judgment, and contextual understanding; computers excel at processing speed, pattern recognition in large datasets, and consistency. The most powerful outcomes come from thoughtfully combining these complementary strengths.
