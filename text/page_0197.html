Endnotes: Barksdale's Witticisms

Commentary

Summary

Endnote 37 describes how Bob Lisbonne, a former SVP at Netscape, compiled a list of Jim Barksdale's witticisms from meetings and posted them on his personal website. Barksdale, as Netscape's CEO, was known for memorable one-liners that captured management wisdom, including the famous quote about facts versus opinions.

Key Patterns

Barksdale's collected witticisms show how memorable phrases can encode management philosophy and travel through organizations. His most famous quote -- "If we have data, let's look at data. If all we have are opinions, let's go with mine" -- encapsulates the book's emphasis on data over hierarchy.

Practical Applications

Develop clear, memorable phrases that encode your leadership principles -- they spread through organizations faster than memos. Default to data in decision-making; when data is unavailable, be explicit about whose judgment takes precedence and why.
