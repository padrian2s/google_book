Commentary

Summary

Endnote 91: Marissa Mayer and Stanford recruiting. Marissa Mayer originally saw this phrase on a recruiting flyer in the CS building when she was a student at Stanford. The phrase caught her eye and she brought it with her to Google.

Key Patterns

Small cultural artifacts -- a phrase on a flyer, a motto on a wall -- can carry outsized influence when they resonate with the right people. Google's early culture was built from such fragments of inspiration.

Practical Applications

Pay attention to the messages your organization broadcasts, even informally. The phrases, slogans, and values visible in your workspace signal who you are and who you want to attract.
