Entering China: The Debate

Commentary

Summary

The decision to enter China was contentious from the start. Sergey Brin was squarely in the "stay out" camp -- his family had immigrated from the Soviet Union and he had firsthand experience with Communist regimes. But many others on Eric's staff disagreed, and business factors plus the hope of changing China's information climate tipped the scale. Sukhinder Singh Cassidy set up operations quickly, establishing a Google China subsidiary. They grudgingly decided to comply with censorship regulations but with a twist: they would inform users when results were being blocked. They couldn't access censored information, but at least they'd know censorship was occurring. Surprisingly, many censorship requests were intended to suppress content that didn't violate any written law -- often government departments censoring each other's statements or suppressing planted scandals.

Key Patterns

The China entry decision showcases the tension between pragmatic compromise and principled absolutism. Sergey's personal history shaped his instinctive opposition -- a reminder that leaders' backgrounds profoundly influence their strategic instincts. The transparency twist (informing users about censorship) was an attempt to find a middle ground between full compliance and full defiance. The revelation about arbitrary censorship requests (agencies censoring each other, suppressing non-illegal content) reveals how authoritarian information control often has no principled basis, making compliance even more morally ambiguous.

Practical Applications

When entering ethically complex markets, look for creative compromises that maintain as much of your values as possible. Build in transparency mechanisms even when compliance is required. Recognize that personal experiences of team members (like Sergey's Soviet background) are not biases to be overcome but valuable perspectives to be weighed. Document the ethical reasoning behind difficult decisions so they can be revisited as circumstances change.
