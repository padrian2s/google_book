Endnotes: Shakespeare's Smart Creative

Commentary

Summary

Endnote 28 is a brief, playful reference: "With apologies to that notable smart creative, Puck." This alludes to the character Puck from Shakespeare's A Midsummer Night's Dream, known for his cleverness, mischief, and creativity. The authors humorously connect Shakespeare's trickster figure to their concept of the "smart creative."

Key Patterns

The authors weave cultural references from Shakespeare to philosophy throughout the book, reflecting the broad intellectual curiosity they value in smart creatives. Brief, witty asides like this one reflect Google's culture of not taking itself too seriously while making serious points.

Practical Applications

Recognize that creativity and a bit of mischief are valuable traits in innovative employees -- the best smart creatives often challenge conventions. Use humor and cultural references in your communications to make ideas memorable and to signal intellectual breadth.
