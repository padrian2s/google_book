Endnotes (Note 122)

Commentary

Summary

Endnote 122: Wolfinger on anecdotes and data. Wolfinger's comment at Stanford in 1969/1970 was a rejoinder to a student who had dismissed data-based findings as 'mere anecdote.' The plural of anecdote is data.

Key Patterns

The tension between anecdotal evidence and statistical analysis is central to decision-making. Google's data-driven culture emerged from recognizing that systematic data collection beats individual stories.

Practical Applications

When someone dismisses evidence as anecdotal, ask whether systematically collecting such observations might reveal meaningful patterns. Good data often starts as carefully observed anecdotes.
