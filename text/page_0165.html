The acknowledgments conclude with thanks to academic advisors including Professor David Teece, family members and friends who reviewed the manuscript, Nishant Choksi for the illustrations, Melissa Thomas for fact-checking, Marina Krakovsky for research, David Javerbaum for humor, and Jim Levine (agent) and John Brodie (editor). Key Patterns

The closing anecdote about childhood friendship coming full circle at Google perfectly embodies the book's title theme: the unimaginable can happen. The collaborative nature of the acknowledgments -- spanning academics, fact-checkers, humor writers, illustrators, researchers, and family -- reflects the book's core principle that great work requires diverse, talented teams working together across traditional boundaries.

Practical Applications

Surround yourself with people who bring diverse skills -- researchers, creatives, editors, fact-checkers. Maintain long-term relationships; you never know where they will lead decades later. Apply "imagine the unimaginable" to your own career and business trajectory. The best outcomes are often ones nobody could have predicted.
