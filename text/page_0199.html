Endnotes: Computer Science Inside Joke

Commentary

Summary

Endnote 39 is a self-deprecating aside: "Computer science inside joke proudly written by Jonathan Rosenberg, economics major. Next footnote." Rosenberg playfully acknowledges his non-technical background while claiming credit for a technical joke, exemplifying the interdisciplinary culture the book advocates.

Key Patterns

Rosenberg's joke about being an economics major writing computer science humor reflects Google's culture of intellectual playfulness across disciplines. The fact that a non-engineer rose to SVP at Google underscores the book's point that smart creatives combine diverse skills rather than fitting narrow technical categories.

Practical Applications

Encourage cross-disciplinary curiosity -- leaders do not need to be experts in every domain but should engage authentically with fields outside their specialty. Use humor, including self-deprecation, to build rapport across teams with different technical backgrounds.
