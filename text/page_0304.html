Endnotes (Note 142)

Commentary

Summary

Endnote 142 provides background on the red and green paddles used at Google's TGIF meetings. Originally featured in the movie The Internship, they were adopted for real TGIF meetings after the movie's release and remained popular for about a year before being upgraded to a digital version so remote employees could participate as well.

Key Patterns

This anecdote illustrates Google's playful culture and willingness to adopt ideas from unexpected sources -- even a Hollywood movie about their own company. The evolution from physical paddles to digital voting also reflects Google's consistent drive to make communication tools more inclusive and scalable for a growing, distributed workforce.

Practical Applications

Create interactive feedback mechanisms in company-wide meetings to keep employees engaged. As your organization grows and becomes more distributed, invest in digital tools that allow remote participants to engage just as actively as those physically present, ensuring inclusivity in company culture.
