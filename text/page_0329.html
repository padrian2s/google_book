Endnotes (Note 167)

Commentary

Summary

Endnote 167 provides a brief clarification that DARPA (Defense Advanced Research Projects Agency) is part of the US Department of Defense. This context is important for understanding DARPA's role as a model for ambitious, mission-driven research and development that the authors reference in their discussion of innovation.

Key Patterns

The authors cite DARPA as an exemplar of how to organize for breakthrough innovation. Despite being a government agency, DARPA has consistently produced transformative technologies (including the Internet itself) through a unique model of empowering small teams, setting audacious goals, and accepting high failure rates. This model parallels many of the principles the authors advocate for corporate innovation.

Practical Applications

Study DARPA's organizational model for lessons in driving breakthrough innovation: small empowered teams, rotating leadership with fixed terms, ambitious challenge-driven goals, and a culture that embraces calculated risk. These principles can be adapted for corporate settings to create more effective innovation programs.
