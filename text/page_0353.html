Endnotes -- Underpromise, Overdeliver

Commentary

Summary

This endnote (191) connects to the concept of "Underpromise, Overdeliver," popularized by Tom Peters in his book "Thriving on Chaos: Handbook for a Management Revolution" (1988). The principle advocates setting expectations conservatively and then exceeding them.

Key Patterns

The "underpromise, overdeliver" principle ties together several themes in the book: managing expectations, building trust through consistent delivery, and maintaining credibility with stakeholders. In a company like Google that sets audacious goals (moonshots), this principle helps manage the tension between ambitious vision and realistic execution.

Practical Applications

When setting timelines and deliverables, build in realistic buffers. It is far better to pleasantly surprise stakeholders with early or better-than-expected delivery than to disappoint with missed deadlines or incomplete work. This builds a track record of reliability that earns you more freedom and trust over time.
