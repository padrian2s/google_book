Endnotes: Shakespeare Reference

Commentary

Summary

Endnote 43 is the briefest endnote in the book: "Shakespeare, William." This deadpan attribution follows a well-known Shakespeare quote used in the main text, demonstrating the authors' characteristic dry humor.

Key Patterns

The extreme brevity of this citation is deliberately funny -- everyone knows who Shakespeare is, making the formal attribution absurdly unnecessary. The authors assume their readers will appreciate the joke, reflecting the intellectual culture they advocate for.

Practical Applications

Use humor strategically in professional communications to create memorable moments and build rapport with your audience. Know when less is more -- sometimes the shortest response is the most effective.
