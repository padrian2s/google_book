Commentary

Summary

Endnote 108: Tournament theory explains outsized compensation. Tournament theory explains how fields set up as long-running contests for top slots use outsize pay as winners' prizes. See Lazear and Rosen's foundational 1981 paper on rank-order tournaments.

Key Patterns

Understanding tournament theory helps explain why top performers in technology command extraordinary compensation. The structure of competition itself drives pay inequality.

Practical Applications

Design compensation structures that reward outsized contributions. In creative and technical fields, the gap between average and exceptional performance justifies significant pay differentials.
