Endnotes: Technologies in Primitive State

Commentary

Summary

Endnote 64 credits the observation that "new technologies come into the world in a primitive state" to Jonathan Rosenberg's father, Nathan Rosenberg, a distinguished economic historian. The source is Nathan Rosenberg's Perspectives on Technology (Cambridge University Press, 1976). This insight suggests that technologies should be judged by their trajectory, not their current state.

Key Patterns

Judging new technologies by their primitive initial form rather than their potential is a common strategic error -- the authors argue for evaluating trajectory over current state. Jonathan drawing on his father's academic work demonstrates how intellectual traditions cross generations. Nathan Rosenberg's economic history provides a long-term lens for understanding technology adoption patterns.

Practical Applications

When evaluating new technologies, assess their improvement trajectory rather than dismissing them based on current limitations. Draw on diverse intellectual traditions -- economic history, in this case -- to inform your technology and business strategy.
