Endnotes: Birth of a Salesman

Commentary

Summary

Endnote 41 cites Richard L. Brandt's "Birth of a Salesman" (Wall Street Journal, October 15, 2011), an article about Steve Jobs's sales acumen and how he transformed product launches into cultural events. This reference supports the book's discussion of how great products and compelling storytelling work together.

Key Patterns

Jobs's ability to sell was rooted in genuine product passion, not traditional salesmanship -- aligning with the book's product-first philosophy. The authors continue to draw lessons from Apple alongside Google, showing that product excellence transcends any single company's approach.

Practical Applications

Build products so good that selling them becomes a natural extension of sharing something you genuinely believe in. Study how the best product leaders communicate their vision -- the ability to articulate product value is as important as building the product itself.
