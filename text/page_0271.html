Endnotes (Note 109)

Commentary

Summary

Endnote 109: The case against egalitarian pay. Egalitarian pay systems, where people making vastly different contributions are paid similarly, may demoralize high performers. The authors believe rewarding top performers outweighs team morale concerns.

Key Patterns

The debate between egalitarian and performance-based pay reflects a fundamental tension in organizational design between fairness and meritocracy. Google's culture explicitly favors meritocratic compensation.

Practical Applications

Be transparent about your compensation philosophy. If you believe in performance-based pay, communicate clearly that outsized contributions will be recognized with outsized rewards.
