Commentary

Summary

Endnote 118: Accounting metrics humor. The authors have nothing against accountants, who have good reasons for calculating pro forma revenues and EBITDA (earnings before interest, taxes, depreciation, and amortization).

Key Patterns

This tongue-in-cheek note reveals the tension between engineering-driven product thinking and financial metrics-driven management. Google's culture prioritizes user value over short-term financial optimization.

Practical Applications

Understand financial metrics but don't let them drive product decisions prematurely. The best products create user value first and figure out monetization second.
