Commentary

Summary

Endnote 135: Aristotle's rhetoric and Oprah's emotional intelligence. This persuasion rule traces from Aristotle's logos, ethos, and pathos to Oprah Winfrey: 'You have to tell the story so that people feel something. They only want to do something after they feel something.'

Key Patterns

Effective persuasion requires engaging logic, establishing credibility, and creating emotional connection. The best communicators -- from ancient philosophers to modern media figures -- integrate all three.

Practical Applications

When presenting ideas or proposals, address all three dimensions of persuasion: make the logical case, establish your credibility to speak on the topic, and connect emotionally with your audience's values and aspirations.
