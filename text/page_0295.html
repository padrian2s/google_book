Endnotes (Note 133)

Commentary

Summary

Endnote 133: Soccer goalkeeper action bias. Research shows soccer goalkeepers exhibit action bias during penalty kicks -- they almost always dive rather than staying in the center, even though staying put would be more effective. This parallels action bias in investment decisions.

Key Patterns

Action bias -- the tendency to do something rather than nothing -- is a common trap in management. Sometimes the best strategic response is disciplined inaction or patience.

Practical Applications

Before taking action on a problem, ask whether action is truly warranted or whether you are responding to the psychological pressure to 'do something.' Strategic patience is an undervalued leadership skill.
