Endnotes (Note 72)

Commentary

Summary

Endnote 72: Nathan Rosenberg on innovation. Nathan Rosenberg pointed out the valuable role companies play in applying existing technologies in new ways. He proposed that a good definition of innovation is something that 'establishes a new framework for the working out of incremental innovations,' which often leads to an entirely new industry.

Key Patterns

Innovation is not just about inventing something entirely new -- it can also mean applying existing technologies in novel ways. This reframing broadens who can be an innovator within an organization.

Practical Applications

Look for opportunities to apply existing technologies from adjacent industries to your own domain. Some of the most impactful innovations come from novel combinations rather than pure invention.
