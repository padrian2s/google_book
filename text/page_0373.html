Endnotes -- Information Rules and Network Economics

Commentary

Summary

This endnote (211) references Carl Shapiro and Hal R. Varian's influential book "Information Rules: A Strategic Guide to the Network Economy" (Harvard Business Review Press, 1998). The book provides foundational frameworks for understanding the economics of information goods, network effects, and platform strategies.

Key Patterns

Shapiro and Varian's work is one of the intellectual foundations of the strategy described in "How Google Works." Their analysis of network effects, lock-in, switching costs, and information economics directly informs the platform-first thinking that the authors advocate. Notably, Hal Varian later became Google's chief economist, bringing these ideas directly into the company's strategic decision-making.

Practical Applications

Study the economics of information goods and network effects. Understanding concepts like lock-in, switching costs, versioning, and bundling is essential for anyone building technology businesses. "Information Rules" remains one of the most practical guides to these concepts, despite being published in 1998.
