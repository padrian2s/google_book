Endnotes: The Faster Horses Myth

Commentary

Summary

Endnote 65 investigates the famous Henry Ford "faster horses" quote and finds it may be apocryphal. In Ford's actual book My Life and Work, he says nothing about faster horses. People had already been discussing horseless carriages for years, and Karl Benz built the first gasoline-powered car in 1885, which Ford saw and disparaged. The witty conclusion: "So perhaps Henry Ford wasn't looking for faster horses, but rather a faster Benz."

Key Patterns

The authors fact-check a widely cited quote and find it wanting -- modeling the data-driven skepticism they advocate. Many business "wisdom" quotes are apocryphal, and the real story is often more nuanced and instructive than the myth. The "faster Benz" punchline demonstrates their characteristic wit even during serious source analysis.

Practical Applications

Verify the sources of popular business wisdom before building strategy on them -- many widely cited quotes and frameworks are oversimplified or misattributed. The real history of innovation is usually more complex and more instructive than the popular narrative -- dig deeper.
