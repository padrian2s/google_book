Endnotes: IBM's Turnaround

Commentary

Summary

Endnote 52 cites Louis V. Gerstner Jr.'s Who Says Elephants Can't Dance?: Inside IBM's Historic Turnaround (HarperBusiness, 2002), page 183. Gerstner's account of transforming IBM from a struggling giant into a competitive force is one of the most celebrated corporate turnaround stories, relevant to the book's discussion of how large organizations can regain agility.

Key Patterns

Gerstner proved that even massive, bureaucratic organizations can be revitalized -- a counterpoint to the assumption that only startups can be innovative. Gerstner famously said "culture isn't just one aspect of the game -- it is the game," directly supporting the book's emphasis on culture.

Practical Applications

Do not assume that organizational size prevents transformation -- with the right leadership and cultural changes, even large companies can become agile. Prioritize culture change as the foundation of any strategic transformation, not an afterthought.
