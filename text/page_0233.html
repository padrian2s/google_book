Commentary

Summary

Endnote 73: Hedgehog Concept and specialization. Jim Collins calls the idea of specializing 'the Hedgehog Concept,' borrowed from Isaiah Berlin's essay. Collins found that great companies in Good to Great were all hedgehogs. However, specializing is a risky strategy that can lead to either great gains or great losses.

Key Patterns

The tension between focus (hedgehog) and adaptability (fox) is a recurring strategic challenge. Google's approach of deep focus on search while expanding into adjacent areas reflects a nuanced version of this concept.

Practical Applications

When defining your company's strategy, identify one core strength to build around, but remain adaptable enough to evolve as technology and markets change. Pure specialization carries risks.
