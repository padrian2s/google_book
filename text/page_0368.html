Endnotes -- Maintaining the Silicon Valley Vision

Commentary

Summary

This endnote (206) references Vinod Khosla's New York Times Bits blog post "Maintain the Silicon Valley Vision" (July 13, 2012). Khosla argues for preserving the bold, risk-taking culture that has made Silicon Valley a global center of innovation.

Key Patterns

Khosla's call to maintain the Silicon Valley vision connects to the book's broader theme about the importance of culture in driving innovation. As companies and regions grow and mature, there is a natural tendency toward risk aversion and incrementalism. Actively maintaining a culture of bold ambition requires deliberate effort and vigilance against complacency.

Practical Applications

Actively protect and nurture your organization's innovative culture, especially as it grows. Success breeds conservatism, so establish mechanisms -- such as moonshot projects, innovation labs, or dedicated time for experimentation -- that keep the entrepreneurial spirit alive even as the company scales.
