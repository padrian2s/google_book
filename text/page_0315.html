Endnotes (Note 153)

Commentary

Summary

Endnote 153 provides background on Calafia, the restaurant where Steve Jobs and Eric Schmidt chose to meet. It is owned by chef Charlie Ayers, Google's first chef (the campus cafe is named after him). However, the authors note the practical reason for the meeting location was its proximity to Steve Jobs' home, not the Google connection.

Key Patterns

This note reveals the personal, informal nature of high-level business relationships in Silicon Valley. Major strategic discussions between leaders of the world's most valuable companies happen in casual settings, reflecting the tech industry's preference for substance over formality. The Google-Calafia connection also illustrates the tight-knit network of Silicon Valley.

Practical Applications

Choose meeting environments that facilitate open, honest conversation rather than formal corporate settings. Important relationships are often built and maintained through informal interactions. Make it easy for the other person to meet by choosing convenient locations, showing respect for their time and circumstances.
