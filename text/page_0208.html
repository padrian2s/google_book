Endnotes: Optimistic Graduates

Commentary

Summary

Endnote 48 cites Steve Friess's "In Recession, Optimistic College Graduates Turn Down Jobs" (New York Times, July 24, 2009). This article documented the surprising phenomenon of new graduates declining job offers during the 2008-2009 recession, choosing to hold out for roles that better matched their aspirations.

Key Patterns

Even during a recession, the most talented candidates prioritized meaningful work over any available job -- confirming the book's thesis that smart creatives have different motivations. When candidates are selective even in downturns, the quality of your organization's mission and culture becomes a competitive advantage in recruiting.

Practical Applications

Do not assume economic downturns make hiring easier for top talent -- the best candidates remain selective regardless of market conditions. Invest in your employer brand and mission clarity; these attract the candidates who will drive the most value.
