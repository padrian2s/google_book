Endnotes (Note 79)

Commentary

Summary

Endnote 79: Apple Macintosh as closed system vs. IBM openness. The Apple Macintosh launched in 1984 as a closed system. Bill Gates noted the IBM architecture had 100 times the engineering resources when compatible manufacturers were included.

Key Patterns

The open vs. closed platform debate has been central to tech strategy for decades. The IBM PC's openness enabled massive ecosystem growth, while Apple's control enabled quality but limited market share.

Practical Applications

Consider the trade-offs between open and closed ecosystems carefully. Openness drives adoption and ecosystem innovation; control enables quality and coherent user experience.
