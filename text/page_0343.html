Endnotes (Note 181)

Commentary

Summary

Endnote 181 clarifies the distinction between "20 percent time" and the "70/20/10" resource allocation model. Twenty percent time is about individual freedom -- allowing engineers to spend a portion of their time on self-directed projects. The 70/20/10 framework is about organizational resource management -- allocating 70% to core business, 20% to adjacent opportunities, and 10% to transformational bets. The authors humorously note that adopting "70/19/11" might have been less confusing.

Key Patterns

The distinction between individual and organizational innovation frameworks is important. Twenty percent time empowers individuals, while 70/20/10 provides strategic portfolio allocation. Both serve the goal of fostering innovation, but they operate at different levels and serve different purposes. Together, they create a system where innovation is both a bottom-up individual activity and a top-down strategic priority.

Practical Applications

Implement both individual and organizational innovation frameworks. At the individual level, give people time and permission to explore their own ideas. At the organizational level, deliberately allocate resources across a portfolio of core, adjacent, and transformational initiatives. Make sure the two frameworks complement rather than conflict with each other, and communicate clearly about the purpose and scope of each.
