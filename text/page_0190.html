Endnotes: Free Food Culture at HP

Commentary

Summary

Endnote 30 reveals that HP pioneered the free food tradition long before Google made it famous. HP employee John Minck recalled how production line donut and Danish trays were heated using soldering irons with variable power transformers. These company-furnished breaks "used to amaze customers" touring the plants. Quoted from Michael Malone's Bill and Dave: How Hewlett and Packard Built the World's Greatest Company (Portfolio/Penguin, 2007), page 130.

Key Patterns

Decades before Google's legendary cafeterias, HP understood that providing food communicates care for employees and impresses visitors -- a small investment with outsized cultural returns. Using soldering irons to heat pastries exemplifies the engineer's mindset of solving every problem creatively. Google's employee perks trace a direct lineage to HP's practices, showing how cultural innovations propagate through Silicon Valley.

Practical Applications

Small perks like free food signal that you value your employees' well-being and create informal gathering spaces that spark collaboration. Look for ways to apply your team's core skills creatively even to mundane problems -- it reinforces identity and builds culture. Consider what impression your workplace makes on visitors; the small details often communicate your values most powerfully.
