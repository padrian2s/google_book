Conclusion: Transportation, Finance, and Creative Industries

Commentary

Summary

Transportation will be transformed when every car can drive itself -- ownership models will change, personal car services will drop in price, and the only reason to own a car will be for pleasure. In financial services, detailed data means more customized services -- auto insurers already use distance driven and location to assess risk. In creative industries, there is more outstanding content and talent than ever before, and technology has created new ways to enjoy storytelling on any device. The Internet has decimated traditional media business models but new ones are emerging, creating a bigger, more fragmented, and more consumer-friendly market.

Key Patterns

Every industry will be transformed by data and technology in the first half of the twenty-first century. Disruption simultaneously destroys old business models and creates new ones. The net result is typically more choice for consumers and more opportunity for creators, but painful transitions for incumbents.

Practical Applications

Examine how data and automation will transform your industry's core assumptions. Prepare for shifts in ownership and consumption models. Embrace fragmentation as an opportunity for specialized, high-quality offerings.
