Endnotes (Note 145)

Commentary

Summary

Endnote 145 is a humorous aside in which the authors mention that Jonathan kept telling his wife a particular point from the main text, but she told him to stop after the fourth repetition. This light-hearted note reflects the casual, self-deprecating tone the authors use throughout the book.

Key Patterns

The authors regularly inject humor and personal anecdotes into what could otherwise be dry business advice. This pattern of mixing serious management insights with self-deprecating humor makes the content more memorable and relatable, demonstrating that even senior executives at major companies maintain a sense of humor about themselves.

Practical Applications

Use humor and personal stories to make your message more engaging and memorable. However, know when your audience has heard enough -- repetition has diminishing returns, and good communicators know when to move on to the next point rather than belaboring a message.
