Introduction: From Students to Teachers

Commentary

Summary

The authors describe how they had to reinvent management rules while growing Google at a breakneck pace. They kept notes from staff meetings, product reviews, and internal memos, gradually building a framework for managing in the new era. This led to talks and management seminars for Google leaders, where they shared "tribal wisdom" about managing smart creatives. Eric proposed they write a book together (joking that Jonathan would do all the work while he got all the credit). The inspiration came from Cisco CEO John Chambers, who described how HP CEO Lew Platt had mentored him, explaining "This is the way Silicon Valley works. We're here to help you." Steve Jobs expressed similar sentiments through a Schopenhauer quote about witnessing the conjurer's tricks.

Key Patterns

The Silicon Valley tradition of knowledge sharing between companies and generations of leaders. The value of capturing institutional knowledge through notes and frameworks over time rather than creating them from scratch. The "conjurer" metaphor -- experienced leaders can see patterns that repeat across generations of companies. The progression from being students of the Google model to being teachers of it.

Practical Applications

Document what you learn about management as you go -- scribble notes, save emails, capture insights in real time. Seek mentorship from experienced leaders, especially those outside your company. Share your management insights broadly rather than hoarding them as competitive advantages. Build internal knowledge-sharing programs that pass wisdom from experienced leaders to the next generation.
