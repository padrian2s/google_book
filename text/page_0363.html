Endnotes -- The Fall of Borders Bookstores

Commentary

Summary

This endnote (201) references the Wall Street Journal article "Bookseller Borders Begins a New Chapter... 11" by Joseph Checkler and Jeffrey A. Trachtenberg (February 17, 2011), reporting on Borders Group filing for Chapter 11 bankruptcy. The clever headline underscores the irony of a bookseller's demise being described in literary terms.

Key Patterns

Borders' bankruptcy is a cautionary tale of a company that failed to adapt to technological disruption. While Amazon built a digital platform, Borders outsourced its online sales to Amazon -- effectively handing its future to a competitor. This exemplifies the book's warnings about companies that fail to bet on technical insights and build their own platforms.

Practical Applications

Never outsource your core strategic capabilities, especially in technology. Borders' decision to let Amazon handle its online business was a fatal strategic error. Identify which technological capabilities are essential to your future and invest in building them in-house, even if outsourcing seems more efficient in the short term.
