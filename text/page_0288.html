Endnotes (Note 126)

Commentary

Summary

Endnote 126: Data reduces interpersonal conflict in teams. Eisenhardt et al. found that reliance on objective, up-to-date facts reduces interpersonal conflict in management teams by keeping focus on issues rather than personalities.

Key Patterns

Data-driven decision-making is not just about better outcomes -- it also creates healthier team dynamics by depersonalizing disagreements and grounding debates in shared facts.

Practical Applications

When facing contentious decisions, invest in gathering comprehensive, objective data before the discussion. This shifts the conversation from 'who is right' to 'what does the evidence show.'
