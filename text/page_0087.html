Google's Hiring Dos and Don'ts

Commentary

Summary

The chapter concludes with a summary of Google's hiring principles in Do/Don't pairs: Hire people smarter and more knowledgeable than you; don't hire people you can't learn from. Hire people who will add value to the product and culture; don't hire those who won't contribute to both. Hire people who will get things done; don't hire people who just think about problems. Hire enthusiastic, self-motivated, and passionate people; don't hire people who just want a job. Hire people who inspire and work well with others; don't hire those who prefer to work alone. Hire people who will grow with the team and company; don't hire people with narrow skill sets. Hire well-rounded people with unique interests and talents; don't hire people who only live to work. Hire ethical people who communicate openly; don't hire political or manipulative people. Hire only when you've found a great candidate; don't settle for anything less.

Key Patterns

The Dos and Don'ts distill the entire Talent chapter into actionable principles. Several patterns emerge: the emphasis on growth potential over current ability, the balance between individual excellence and collaborative orientation, the requirement for both professional and personal breadth, and the absolute refusal to compromise standards. The final pair -- hire only great candidates, never settle -- is the ultimate expression of the chapter's core argument. The combination of product contribution and cultural contribution ensures that new hires strengthen both the company's output and its internal dynamics.

Practical Applications

Use these Dos and Don'ts as a hiring checklist for every candidate evaluation. Post them prominently in hiring committee rooms. Train interviewers to assess candidates against each dimension. When you feel pressure to compromise on any of these principles, remember that the long-term cost of a mediocre hire far exceeds the short-term cost of an unfilled position. The most important principle: never settle for anything less than a great candidate.
